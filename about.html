HARA SHIN (신하라/申荷羅)

Hara Shin (b. 1987 in Seoul, lives and works in Berlin) works with video and installation that investigates alternative experiences, sensory perception of various (im)material properties arising from media-based observation and speculation. The structure of image circulation and the flowing path of visibility are multi-layered in a space that presents hypotheses, in or out of a digital environment. Cognitive space and real space react to each other’s absences, influence the sphere of action, and present the possibility of drifting in space. Leaving behind the concept of the modernity of human-centered thinking and the future illusion of advanced humanity, She explores relationships and traces of natural life and artificiality from a fragmented perspective. The interconnections between objects and their surroundings over time change between their death and disappearance, and recurrent existential values. She graduated as Meisterschülerin of Nina Fischer in Experimental Film & Media Art class at the University of Arts Berlin.
